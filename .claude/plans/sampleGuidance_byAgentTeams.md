## Guidance Style

Apple does **not** provide traditional dollar-range revenue guidance. Their standard guidance framework consists of:

| Metric | Format | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Revenue | Qualitative YoY growth rate | "low to mid single digits" |
| Gross Margin | Percentage range (100bps wide) | "46% to 47%" |
| Operating Expenses | Dollar range ($200M wide) | "$15.3B to $15.5B" |
| Tax Rate | Single percentage | "around 16%" |
| OINE | Single dollar estimate | "around negative $250M" |
| Services | Directional growth rate | "low double digits YoY" |
| Segments | Sometimes directional | "accelerate / decelerate" |

**Key characteristics:**
- Guidance is provided only for the immediately following quarter; Apple explicitly states they "don't forecast beyond the current quarter"
- All guidance is conditional on "macroeconomic outlook not worsening" from the date of the call
- Starting Q2 FY2025, tariff-related caveats were added assuming "current global tariff rates, policies, and applications remain in effect"
- Revenue guidance assumes the current Google revenue-share agreement continues (noted starting Q3 FY2025)
- CFO transition: **Luca Maestri** through Q4 FY2024; **Kevin Parekh** starting Q1 FY2025
- **Historical note**: In April 2022 (Q2 FY2022 call), Apple explicitly announced it was "Not Providing Revenue Guidance Going Forward," resuming only qualitative directional guidance. This makes the recent shift to specific percentage ranges (Q4 FY2025 onward) a notable format change.

**Structural margin insight** (from Luca Maestri, Q4 FY2023 Q&A with Krish Sankar, TD Cowen):
> "One of the things that we've done well over the last few years is to offer more affordability solutions to our customers in the form of installment plans, trading options, and low cost financing in general. And what that has accomplished is reduced the affordability threshold for our customers. And therefore they can buy at the top of our product ranges. That has been a big factor in the reason for our margin expansion."

This explains why GM guidance is systematically conservative: product-mix drift toward premium models is a tailwind that management can observe but won't quantify in forward guidance.

- **Additional Q&A color** (Michael Ng, Goldman Sachs): Kevin Parekh confirmed CapEx growth is driven by "a combination of factors" — AI investment (including Private Cloud Compute in first-party data centers), a hybrid strategy using both first-party and third-party capital, plus periodic investments in facilities and tooling. - **Sources**: Transcript (prepared remarks + Q&A)

#### Revenue Guide Bridge (Q&A detail)
- **Analyst challenge** (Ben Reitzes, Melius Research): Asked why revenue would decelerate from Q3's 10% to "mid-to-high single digits" given services staying at 13% and favorable FX.
- **CFO response** (Kevin Parekh): Two factors explain the step-down: (1) ~1pp of Q3's 10pp growth was tariff-related pull-ahead demand concentrated in April; (2) the September quarter a year ago had full-quarter impact of iPad launches, creating a difficult compare. FX is "a very minor tailwind going from Q3 to Q4, so not really a major factor."
- **Trading implication**: This bridge makes the "mid-to-high single digits" guidance more modelable — stripping the 1pp pull-ahead and iPad comp effect from Q3's 10%, the underlying trend supports the high end of the guided range.

#### Qualitative Guidance
- Guidance conditional on: global tariff rates staying in effect, macro not worsening, Google revenue share agreement continuing
- ~1pp of 10pp total revenue growth attributable to tariff-driven pull-ahead demand in April
- Progress on personalized Siri; features expected "next year"
- Significantly growing AI investments

- **Trading implication**: Supply chain reshoring costs are already embedded in guided margins — not a future headwind.
