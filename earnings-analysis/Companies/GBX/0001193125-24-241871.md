## Executive Summary

**Why did GBX stock rise +16.48% (adjusted)?**

Greenbrier delivered a massive Q4 EPS beat of +48.84% ($1.92 vs $1.29 consensus), driven by exceptional gross margin expansion to 18.2% (vs 15.1% prior quarter). The company achieved its long-term margin targets two years ahead of schedule while generating the second-highest quarterly EBITDA in company history. Despite FY2025 revenue guidance coming in slightly below consensus (-3.58%), investors rewarded the dramatic profitability improvement and management's confirmation that FY2025 EPS should remain "close to $5."

## Attribution

### Primary Driver
**Massive EPS Beat (+48.84%)**
- Surprise: Expected $1.29, Got $1.92 (+$0.63 or +48.84%)
- Evidence: News headline "Greenbrier Companies Q4 2024 EPS $1.92 Beats $1.29 Estimate, Sales $1.053B Beat $1.015B Estimate" (2024-10-23)
- Why it matters: The nearly 50% EPS beat reflected exceptional operational execution. Management achieved long-term margin targets (mid-teens aggregate gross margin) two years ahead of their April 2023 Investor Day guidance. This signals sustainable profitability improvement, not a one-time benefit.

### Contributing Factor #1
**Gross Margin Expansion to 18.2%**
- Evidence: "Q4 aggregate gross margin expanded to 18.2%. This is 310 basis points of sequential margin growth." (Source: Transcript Prepared Remarks, Lori Ticorius)
- Impact: This was the highest quarterly gross margin in over six years. Manufacturing gross margin hit 14.8%, reflecting operational improvements, favorable product mix, and strong syndication activity. This amplified the EPS beat and signaled margin sustainability.

### Contributing Factor #2
**Management Confirmation of ~$5 FY2025 EPS**
- Evidence: Analyst Bascom Majors: "it does seem like... there is an opportunity to put EPS in the close to $5 range." CEO Lori Ticorius: "You know, Babcom, this is Lori. I think you're spot on. We came very close in 2024 to being, we're just shy of $5. And I can tell you that the way that this team is operating and executing, I have no doubt that we will continue to show improvements." (Source: Transcript Q&A #1)
- Impact: This confirmation that FY2025 EPS should remain near $5 (or higher) offset concerns about the slight guidance miss on revenue.

### Mitigating Factor (Did NOT derail the rally)
**FY2025 Revenue Guidance Slightly Below Consensus**
- Evidence: "Greenbrier Companies Sees FY2025 Sales $3.350B-3.650B vs $3.63B Est" (Source: News, 2024-10-23)
- Impact: The -3.58% guidance miss (midpoint $3.50B vs $3.63B consensus) was overshadowed by the massive EPS beat and margin expansion. Investors prioritized profitability over top-line growth.

## Historical Context (for future analyses)

**What moves GBX stock:**
1. **Margin expansion is the key lever**: GBX trades on profitability improvement, not just revenue growth. A 310 bps margin improvement drove a 16%+ stock move despite flat/slightly lower guidance.

2. **Forward EPS confirmation matters**: CEO explicitly confirming ~$5 EPS target neutralized guidance concerns. Watch for management tone on earnings trajectory.

3. **Syndication activity is a swing factor**: Q4 benefited from 1,600 unit syndications generating strong margins. This is transactional and can vary; distinguish from "recurring revenue."

4. **Long-term target progress**: GBX achieved April 2023 Investor Day targets (mid-teens margin, ROIC approaching 10-14%) two years early. Future stock moves may depend on whether they set new targets.

5. **Backlog provides visibility**: $3.4B backlog (26,700 units) gives multi-year revenue visibility. Focus on margins within that backlog, not just volume.

**Company-Specific Sensitivities:**
- Aggregate gross margin (target: 16-16.5% for FY2025)
- Recurring revenue growth (doubling target by FY2028)
- Syndication volume (lumpy but margin-accretive)
- Lease fleet utilization (currently ~99%)
