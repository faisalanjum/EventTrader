## Executive Summary

Dell Technologies reported strong Q2 FY2026 results with record revenue ($29.8B, +19% YoY) and EPS beats, yet the stock plunged 9.2% on weak near-term guidance and margin concerns. The company raised full-year revenue guidance by $4B and AI server expectations to $20B+, but Q3 EPS guidance of $2.45 came in 4% below the $2.55 consensus.

**The market reaction reflects a classic "beat but disappoint on guidance" pattern**, where strong current-quarter results were overshadowed by:
1. Near-term margin pressure from aggressive AI server pricing
2. One-time supply chain costs for Blackwell deployments
3. Continued weakness in storage and traditional servers

## Attribution

### PRIMARY DRIVER (High Confidence): Q3 EPS Guidance Miss

**What**: Near-term earnings guidance came in 4% below consensus expectations, signaling margin pressure despite record revenue.

**Surprise**: Expected $2.55, Got $2.45 (-$0.10, -3.92% miss)

**Evidence**:
- "Dell Sees Q3 Adj EPS $2.45 vs $2.55 Est" (Source: News:bzNews_47399568, 2025-08-28)
- Margin compression from AI server mix (50% of ISG at low single-digit margins)
- One-time supply chain expedite costs for GB200 deployments

**Why It Matters**: The market prices stocks on forward expectations. A -4% near-term guidance miss outweighed +1-2% backward-looking beats because it signals margin pressure will persist into H2.

---

### CONTRIBUTING FACTORS

**1. AI Server Margin Compression (Medium Confidence)**
- AI servers were ~50% of ISG revenue in Q2 (3x prior quarter mix)
- Early Blackwell deals priced aggressively to win market share
- "One-time supply chain expedite costs" for GB200 deployments
- Evidence: "Early Blackwell deals were aggressive, very competitive" (Transcript Q&A #3, Jeff Clark)

**2. Storage Weakness (Medium Confidence)**
- Storage revenue: $3.9B (-3% YoY)
- Large accounts slower, particularly in North America
- HCI headwind as customers rethink private cloud
- Evidence: Transcript Q&A #2, Jeff Clark; Storage expected flat vs prior +3% growth guide

**3. Traditional Server/Federal Softness (Low Confidence)**
- North America traditional server demand challenged
- Federal spending continues down
- Evidence: Transcript Q&A #7, Jeff Clark
